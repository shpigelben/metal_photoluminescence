The change in variables in 3D goes as follows
$$
d^{3}k\to 4\pi k^{2} dk \to \underbrace{ 4\pi k^{2} \frac{dk}{d\mathcal{E}} }_{ \rho(\mathcal{E}) } d\mathcal{E} 
$$
the momentum energy relation comes from the dispersion relation of the electron which is approximated to be that of a free electron for a conduction electron in the 1st BZ.

$$
\mathcal{E}(k)=\frac{\hbar^{2}k^{2}}{2m}\quad \to \quad k(\mathcal{E}) = \frac{\sqrt{ 2m\mathcal{E} }}{\hbar} \quad \to \quad \frac{dk}{d\mathcal{E}}=\frac{1}{\hbar}\sqrt{ \frac{m}{2\mathcal{E}} } 
$$
plugging the relevant expressions in $(A2.2)$ into $(A 2.1)$ results in

$$
\rho(\mathcal{E}) = \frac{4\pi {m_e}^{3/2}}{\hbar^3}\sqrt{2\mathcal{E}}
$$
